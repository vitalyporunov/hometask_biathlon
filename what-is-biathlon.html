What is Biathlon?

The Ultimate Test of Endurance and Precision

A Fusion of Skiing and Shooting

Biathlon is a captivating winter sport that combines the endurance of cross-country skiing with the precision of rifle shooting. Athletes race on a cross-country ski course, stopping at designated shooting ranges to fire at targets. Missed shots result in penalty loops or extra time added to their overall race time.

History of Biathlon

Biathlon has its roots in ancient hunting and military traditions. It evolved from military skiing exercises in Scandinavia and eventually became a competitive sport in the 20th century. The first biathlon world championships were held in 1958, and biathlon was included in the Winter Olympics in 1960.

The Rules of Biathlon

Race Format Distance (km) Shooting Stages Penalty for Missed Shot
Individual 20 (men), 15 (women) 4 (2 prone, 2 standing) 1 minute added to time
Sprint 10 (men), 7.5 (women) 2 (1 prone, 1 standing) 150-meter penalty loop
Pursuit 12.5 (men), 10 (women) 4 (2 prone, 2 standing) 150-meter penalty loop
Mass Start 15 (men), 12.5 (women) 4 (2 prone, 2 standing) 150-meter penalty loop
Relay 4 x 7.5 (men), 4 x 6 (women) 2 per leg (1 prone, 1 standing) 150-meter penalty loop
